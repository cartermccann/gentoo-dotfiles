The lesson worth stealing from Apple's walkback

  • Apple spent the entire beta cycle increasing frost and contrast after critics found menus unreadable over bright backgrounds. NN/g called the result a usability regression.
  • The fix that worked was more diffusion, not more transparency. A heavily blurred surface at moderate opacity stays legible; a lightly blurred surface at high transparency does not.
  • They also shipped an escape hatch: a Tinted mode plus Reduce Transparency. Worth mirroring as an atlas-theme variant rather than a one-way commitment.
  • Panel 3 leans on this: high radius so the backdrop becomes light rather than detail, opacity kept at .66, and text over a slightly lifted plate.

Refraction is the part that genuinely cannot be reproduced, and it is also the part that made Liquid Glass distinctive. What is reachable here is a very good frosted glass with a lit edge, which is the half that aged well.
