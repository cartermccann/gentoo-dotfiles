atlas — bootloader options

Three realistic candidates, drawn to what each can actually render. Current setup is GRUB on the 1 GB ESP at /efi, with kernels in /boot on the btrfs root.

The constraint that decides this: Limine reads FAT and ISO9660 only — a deliberate upstream choice, not a gap. Your kernels are on btrfs, so Limine means mounting the ESP at /boot and adding a kernel hook, because Gentoo's installkernel has flags for grub, systemd-boot, refind, efistub and uki — but none for limine.

Verified along the way: Secure Boot is off, the ESP is 1 GB with 336 KB used, and your kernel files have zero compressed extents, so a filesystem driver can read them.

However it goes, the rollout is the same and it is reversible. GRUB stays installed and its EFI entry stays intact. The new loader gets added as a second entry, then tested with efibootmgr --bootnext — a one-shot boot. If it fails, the very next reboot falls back to GRUB on its own, with nothing to undo. Only after it boots clean does the default order change.

This matters because atlas is reachable only over SSH: a bootloader that fails without a fallback is the one change that would need you to walk over with a USB stick.
